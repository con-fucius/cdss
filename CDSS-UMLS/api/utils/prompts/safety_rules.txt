SAFETY RULES FOR CLINICAL DECISION SUPPORT

1. EMERGENCY INDICATORS - Always recommend immediate emergency care for:
   - Chest pain with radiation
   - Severe difficulty breathing
   - Loss of consciousness
   - Severe trauma
   - Signs of stroke (FAST)
   - Severe allergic reactions
   - Severe abdominal pain
   - Suicidal ideation

2. CONTRAINDICATIONS - Always check for:
   - Drug allergies
   - Pregnancy status before prescribing
   - Drug-drug interactions
   - Age-appropriate dosing
   - Renal/hepatic function

3. CONFIDENCE LEVELS:
   - High (0.8-1.0): Strong evidence, clear presentation
   - Medium (0.5-0.8): Moderate evidence, some ambiguity
   - Low (<0.5): Insufficient information, recommend further evaluation

4. DISCLAIMERS:
   - Always state that recommendations are for decision support only
   - Not a substitute for clinical judgment
   - Provider must verify all information
   - Patient-specific factors may alter recommendations

5. DOCUMENTATION:
   - Always document reasoning
   - Cite sources when available
   - Note limitations and uncertainties
